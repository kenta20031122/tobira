# Tobira 90-Day Focus Strategy

## Strategic recommendation

For the next 90 days, Tobira should focus on one user: English-speaking independent travelers planning their second Japan trip, or their first trip beyond the Tokyo-Kyoto-Osaka route.

This is the segment that best fits the current product. They already believe Japan is worth revisiting, they are actively looking for alternatives to mainstream itineraries, and they have enough intent to value curated discovery plus practical planning support.

## Primary ICP

- Profile:
  - English-speaking international traveler
  - Travels independently, not on package tours
  - Already visited Japan once, or feels done with the default first-trip route
  - Plans trips in advance and compares blogs, Reddit, maps, and social content
  - Wants a trip that feels personal, not copied from every other itinerary
- Core pain:
  - Too much fragmented inspiration, not enough trustworthy trip assembly
- Desired outcome:
  - A confident, distinctive Japan itinerary without spending dozens of hours researching

## Best one-line value proposition

Build a beyond-the-guidebook Japan itinerary with locally curated spots and practical AI planning.

## Why this ICP fits the current product

1. The brand already points at this user.
   - `Beyond Tokyo. Real Japan.` is not a broad travel promise. It is a clear signal for travelers who want a less standard second-layer Japan trip.
2. The current product solves both discovery and planning.
   - Tobira already has guides, blog content, a discovery quiz, lead capture, and an itinerary planner. That combination is strongest for people who have intent but need help narrowing and assembling.
3. The paid offer matches this user's job to be done.
   - Pro is not positioned as discounts or generic travel booking. It is sold on premium spots, practical details, and unlimited itinerary generation. That fits users paying for confidence and time savings.

## Audiences to ignore for now

- Budget or discount-first travelers looking for airfare, hotel, or OTA-style deals
- Domestic Japanese users as a primary segment
- Luxury concierge travelers expecting white-glove service
- Users who want same-day nearby recommendations more than pre-trip planning
- Broad "anyone visiting Japan" messaging

## What Tobira should be known for

- Better Japan trips, not more content
- Local curation, not scraped lists
- Practical planning confidence, not generic AI chat

## Message hierarchy

- Primary message:
  - Build a beyond-the-guidebook Japan itinerary with locally curated spots and practical AI planning.
- Supporting proof point 1:
  - Curated by locals who have been there, not assembled from generic travel listicles.
- Supporting proof point 2:
  - Helps users move from inspiration to a day-by-day route, not just a saved list of places.
- Supporting proof point 3:
  - Focuses on authentic regional travel across Japan, which matches the needs of repeat and deeper-travel users.

## Main activation event

`planner_completed`

This is the clearest signal that a user understood the value, trusted the product enough to plan with it, and is now close to either saving, sharing, or upgrading.

## Main Pro conversion trigger

Planning confidence.

In practice, that means Pro should be framed as the way to move from "interesting ideas" to "a trip I can actually run" through unlimited planning plus richer access, timing, and insider detail.

## Main funnel bottleneck to focus on first

The gap between anonymous discovery and planner completion.

There are multiple good entry points in the current product, but they spread attention. The most important system-level job is to increase the share of qualified visitors who reach and complete planning.

## Recommended 90-day priorities

1. Unify messaging around one ICP and one promise across home, pricing, blog CTA, and email.
2. Make `discover -> planner` the clearest primary path for high-intent users.
3. Instrument the funnel so the team can see where qualified visitors drop.
4. Run a small number of message and CTA experiments instead of broad product expansion.
5. Prioritize regions and content themes that strengthen the repeat-visitor promise.

## Key risks

- The chosen ICP may be too narrow if most current traffic is actually first-time Japan visitors.
- The product may still need more visible practical planning detail before users perceive enough value to pay.
- SEO can take time, and social traffic may produce weaker intent than expected.
- The planner may be the strongest value feature, but the current flow may still add too much friction before users experience it.

## What would disconfirm this strategy

- User interviews show first-time visitors convert and retain better than repeat visitors.
- Planner completion does not correlate with signup or paid conversion.
- Users say the main value is simple destination discovery, not itinerary confidence.
- Instagram and SEO traffic produce weak planner-start and planner-complete rates even after message alignment.
- Pricing tests show users respond more to premium-spot exclusivity than to planning confidence.
