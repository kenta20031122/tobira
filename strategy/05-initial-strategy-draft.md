# Tobira Initial Strategy Draft

This is the first focused strategy recommendation based on the current repo and product shape. Treat it as a working draft.

## Recommended primary ICP

English-speaking independent travelers planning their second or third Japan trip, before departure, who want a more original itinerary than the Tokyo-Kyoto-Osaka loop but still need practical planning confidence.

## Why this ICP fits the current product

- Tobira already positions itself around `Beyond Tokyo. Real Japan.`
- The product is stronger at curated discovery plus itinerary planning than at first-time Japan education
- Repeat visitors are more likely to value better options, local detail, and planning speed
- The current quiz, blog, guides, and planner are all better at narrowing choices than teaching mainstream basics

## Users to ignore for now

- First-time visitors mainly seeking the standard golden route
- Users looking for the cheapest deals or OTA-like booking flows
- Luxury concierge users expecting manual trip design
- Domestic Japanese travelers as the first core audience

## Recommended value proposition

Plan a more original Japan trip, faster, with locally curated spots and practical itinerary guidance you can actually use.

## Supporting proof points

- Curated by locals rather than scraped from generic travel content
- Discovery quiz quickly narrows down matched destinations
- AI planner turns inspiration into a usable day-by-day route

## Recommended north star

`Planner completions per qualified visitor`

This is the strongest shared metric across product and marketing because it measures both intent and delivered value.

## Recommended main activation event

`planner_completed`

Reason:

- It is a stronger signal than quiz completion
- It means the user received the product's clearest utility output
- It should correlate more directly with signup, saved trip behavior, and paid conversion

## Strongest Pro conversion trigger

Planning confidence, packaged as unlimited itinerary generation plus deeper premium spot context.

Do not position Pro as only "more content". Position it as the product that helps users confidently turn a non-obvious Japan idea into an executable plan.

## Most important funnel bottleneck

The transition from discovery interest to authenticated planner usage.

In the current shape, the user can browse, finish the quiz, unlock by email, and then still hit auth before the strongest value moment arrives. That creates too much friction between curiosity and first useful output.

## 90-day strategic focus

1. Increase the share of qualified visitors who complete a planner run
2. Make the planner the center of the monetization story
3. Focus acquisition on channels that can feed high-intent discovery traffic

## Recommended acquisition channels

### 1. SEO

Best use:

- intent-led blog articles
- region guides
- `without car`, seasonal, and alternative-destination topics

Best landing pages:

- blog articles for problem-aware search
- guide pages for region-aware search
- `/discover` for broader exploratory search

### 2. Instagram / Reels / carousel discovery

Best use:

- visually strong regional lists
- seasonal lists
- car-free and theme-based collections

Best landing pages:

- `/discover` for broad top-of-funnel users
- specific guide pages for region-led content

### 3. Pinterest or similar visual discovery

Best use:

- itinerary inspiration
- map-like region discovery
- seasonal and save-for-later planning content

Best landing pages:

- guides and blog

## Content pillars

- Alternatives to overdone Japan itineraries
- Car-free regional travel
- Seasonal regional planning
- Interest-led discovery: onsen, history, food, nature
- Sample itineraries for specific traveler types

## What not to prioritize yet

- Broad paid acquisition before funnel instrumentation is clearer
- B2B partnerships as a primary growth engine
- Mobile app strategy as a growth priority ahead of repeatable acquisition
- Trying to win first-time Japan travel search head-on

## Three concrete experiments for the next 90 days

### Experiment 1: Bridge the gap from quiz results to planner start

- Hypothesis:
  - Users who finish the quiz are interested, but too many drop before using the planner.
- Change:
  - Make the main post-quiz CTA about building a trip from the matched spots, not just unlocking results.
  - Push users directly to signup with their matched context preserved.
- Success metric:
  - `discover_completed -> planner_started`

### Experiment 2: Reframe Pro around planning confidence

- Hypothesis:
  - Users will pay more for a complete, confident planning workflow than for premium spots alone.
- Change:
  - On pricing and lead email, lead with unlimited planning plus practical insider detail rather than a generic premium bundle.
  - Keep premium spot access as proof, not the headline.
- Success metric:
  - `pricing_viewed -> subscription_started`

### Experiment 3: Build one tight SEO + social topic cluster

- Hypothesis:
  - A focused cluster will outperform broad content scatter.
- Change:
  - Choose one pillar such as `car-free regional Japan` or `second-trip Japan alternatives`.
  - Publish matching blog articles, guide CTAs, and Instagram carousels that all point to the same discovery and planner flow.
- Success metric:
  - Qualified sessions, discover starts, and planner completions from that cluster

## Immediate implementation implications

- Instrument the funnel before expanding channel scope
- Treat planner completion as the core product value event
- Reduce friction between quiz completion, signup, and first planner success
- Keep messaging consistent across homepage, email, pricing, and social
