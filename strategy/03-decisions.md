## Open decisions to resolve first

### DEC-001: Primary ICP for the next 90 days

- Date: 2026-03-20
- Status: Recommended
- Owner: Strategy workspace
- Context:
  - Messaging currently speaks to a broad international traveler audience.
  - Strategy work will be noisy unless one user segment is prioritized.
- Options considered:
  - First-time visitors seeking alternatives to Tokyo/Kyoto
  - Repeat visitors seeking deeper regional travel
  - Time-poor independent travelers who want itinerary help fast
- Decision:
  - Focus on English-speaking independent travelers planning their second Japan trip, or their first trip beyond the Tokyo-Kyoto-Osaka route.
- Why this option:
  - The current brand, guides, blog, premium spots, and planner all fit users who already know the mainstream route and now want better options with less research effort.
- Risks:
  - It may exclude a portion of first-time visitors who are still open to regional travel.
- Revisit when:
  - After 20 user conversations or one full 6-week channel test cycle

### DEC-002: Primary value proposition

- Date: 2026-03-20
- Status: Recommended
- Owner: Strategy workspace
- Context:
  - Tobira has two strong stories: local curation and AI planning.
  - The product needs a clear "hero" promise.
- Options considered:
  - `Curated by locals`
  - `AI itinerary planning`
  - `Hidden gems across Japan`
  - Hybrid positioning
- Decision:
  - Lead with planning confidence built on local curation: `Build a beyond-the-guidebook Japan itinerary with locally curated spots and practical AI planning.`
- Why this option:
  - Curation explains trust. AI explains speed. Together they match the current product better than either claim alone.
- Risks:
  - If the AI angle dominates, the product risks sounding generic. If the curation angle dominates alone, the planner may feel secondary.
- Revisit when:
  - After messaging tests on homepage and top acquisition channels

### DEC-003: Main Pro conversion trigger

- Date: 2026-03-20
- Status: Recommended
- Owner: Strategy workspace
- Context:
  - Pro currently bundles premium spots, insider info, and unlimited AI plans.
  - The team should know which benefit does most of the conversion work.
- Options considered:
  - Unlimited planner usage
  - Premium spot details
  - Practical planning confidence bundle
- Decision:
  - Sell Pro primarily as planning confidence: unlimited itinerary generation plus the deeper practical details needed to actually run the trip.
- Why this option:
  - The current product already bundles planner access, insider tips, maps, and access details. The bundle is stronger than selling "hidden gems" alone.
- Risks:
  - If too much is gated too early, users may not reach enough value before the paywall.
- Revisit when:
  - After pricing page tests and first subscription cohort review

### DEC-004: Primary acquisition channels

- Date: 2026-03-20
- Status: Recommended
- Owner: Strategy workspace
- Context:
  - Too many parallel channels will dilute learning.
- Options considered:
  - SEO
  - Instagram / Reels
  - Pinterest / visual discovery
  - Partnerships
  - Paid ads
- Decision:
  - Prioritize SEO and Instagram. Keep Pinterest as a secondary test only if existing assets can be repurposed cheaply.
- Why this option:
  - The repo already contains blog, guides, and Instagram production systems. These channels fit the current team and product better than broader paid acquisition.
- Risks:
  - SEO is slow, and Instagram traffic quality may vary. If both underperform, learning may take longer than expected.
- Revisit when:
  - After 6 weeks of channel-level KPI review
